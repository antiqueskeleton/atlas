# Atlas Domain Model

## Purpose

The Domain Model defines the vocabulary used throughout Atlas.

All code, documentation, reports, dashboards, and database objects should use these terms consistently.

---

# Market

A collection of related industries, competitors, products, regulations, and buying behavior.

Example:

Portable Power

---

# Category

A subdivision within a Market.

Examples

Portable Generators

Inverter Generators

Battery Backup

Transfer Switches

---

# Persona

A representation of a customer type.

Examples

Homeowner

RV Owner

Contractor

Farmer

Camper

Food Truck Owner

---

# Buying Journey

The path a customer follows.

Awareness

↓

Research

↓

Comparison

↓

Purchase

↓

Ownership

↓

Maintenance

↓

Replacement

---

# Market Question

A real-world question asked by a customer.

Every Market Question may exist in multiple prompt styles.

Search

Natural Language

Conversation

Expert

Shopping

---

# Prompt

One linguistic representation of a Market Question.

A prompt is NOT the primary object.

The Market Question is.

---

# Evidence

The fundamental object inside Atlas.

Evidence represents an observation collected from any source.

Examples

AI response

Review

Article

Manual

Video

Retail listing

News

Forum discussion

---

# Knowledge

Curated information maintained by Atlas.

Knowledge includes

Brands

Products

Features

Prompt Library

Regulations

Retailers

Industry Events

---

# Analyst

An independent analysis module.

Examples

Brand Analyst

Product Analyst

Feature Analyst

Persona Analyst

Opportunity Analyst

---

# Insight

A meaningful conclusion supported by evidence.

Example

Champion visibility increased within RV conversations.

---

# Recommendation

A suggested business action supported by evidence.

Example

Develop additional RV-focused buying content.

---

# Investigation

A structured analysis of a business question.

Example

Why is Honda dominating quiet generator recommendations?

---

# Briefing

A generated executive summary.

Daily

Weekly

Monthly

Quarterly
